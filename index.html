My work

DPhil Research

My research sits at the intersection of climate attribution science and probabilistic forecasting — trying to answer: how much more likely did global warming make this heatwave?

🌡️
Extreme weather attribution
Quantifying how exceedance probabilities for extreme heat events scale with global mean temperature, using reforecast-based tail slope methods.
Core thesis
📊
Reforecast tail slopes
Fitting log-linear slopes (d log P / dT) across climate states using ECMWF reforecast data — validated against Nick Leach's counterfactual ensembles.
Methodology
🔥
Pacific Northwest heatwave
Case study: the 2021 Pacific Northwest event. Risk ratio ~2.2×/°C warming — consistent with counterfactual forecast results at day 11.
Case study
⚙️
HPC & data pipelines
Building Python/xarray/dask pipelines on AOPP HPC to process ECMWF GRIB/NetCDF reforecast files into DJF M-Climate Zarr datasets.
Technical
